## 1. Product Context: What is HZLR?

**HZLR** is a custom-built, full-stack gig-economy platform designed to bridge the gap between temporary workers and employers. Originally prototyped on a low-code stack, HZLR's architecture has been completely rewritten into a highly scalable Node.js/PostgreSQL backend with a React/Vite frontend.

### Core Value Propositions & Features

- **Hybrid QR Attendance System:** Frictionless job site check-ins. Workers scan an employer-generated QR code, capturing precise clock-ins.
- **Interactive Geolocation Mapping:** Resolves ISP location inaccuracies via an interactive, draggable map marker system on the home feed, allowing workers to easily verify their exact gig locations seamlessly.
- **Escrow Wallet System:** A native wallet and escrow engine that securely holds gig funds, assuring workers of guaranteed payment upon job completion, and assuring employers their money is well-spent. 
- **Robust Job Categorization:** 50+ industry-standard job categories designed for granular matching, giving employers hyper-targeted access to the specific skillsets they need.
- **Frictionless Onboarding:** A customized KYC & OTP authentication flow that strikes a balance between necessary security and drop-off prevention, optimized specifically to keep worker sign-ups converting seamlessly.

### Target Demographics
**1. Workers (The Supply):** Freelancers, skilled laborers, and gig-workers looking for fast, reliable work with *guaranteed* payouts via our escrow system.
**2. Employers (The Demand):** Site managers, small business owners, and event organizers who need immediate, trackable (QR check-in), and accountable labor sorted by specific categories.

Instead of static email blasts, implement an **Autonomous Nurture Path**. AI agents monitor intent signals (like when a user abandons the KYC process) and dispatch personalized, dynamic OTP reminders or help materials at the exact statistical moment the user is most likely to convert.

Workers complain about bad UI on other platforms assigning them miles away from actual job sites. HZLR solved this with an interactive map override. Highlight this as a "Built by those who know" UI/UX feature to win grassroots goodwill from actual gig workers.
